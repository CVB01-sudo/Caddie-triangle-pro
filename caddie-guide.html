02 · ON THE GREEN
The read sequence
20 seconds once it's muscle memory. Same order every time.
1
Walk the putt — enter steps
Heel-to-toe from ball to cup. Half steps supported — 7.5 steps = 20.25 ft. Enter distance first, before reading anything else.
2
Find the cup low point
Crouch behind the cup and look straight at it. Which side is lower? Set Left / Center / Right. This drives break direction and activates the proximity factor inside 15ft.
→ Most important single read. Cup low = where the ball enters.
3
Call the slope — exact %
10 precise chips: 0%, 0.5%, 1%, 1.5%, 2%, 2.5%, 3%, 3.5%, 4%, 5%. Call the number like Chad does. When in doubt, go one increment lower — amateurs always over-read.
4
Set uphill or downhill
Uphill kills break (×0.70). Downhill amplifies (×1.35). Flat is baseline (×1.0).
5
Set Capture Speed
Die It — ball barely reaches the hole, falls in soft (+20% break). Normal — standard pace, 12–18" past. Firm — charge the back of the cup (−15% break).
→ Fast downhill: always Die It. Short straight putts: Firm works.
6
Confirm grain (set once)
Shiny surface = grain going away from you (With grain, less break). Dull = Against grain, more break. Across = neutral. Set once on the practice green, leave it.
7
Read the result — commit
App shows aim point in feet/inches, break direction, proximity note if inside 15ft, lag strategy if over 30ft, and Chad's tip. Find the aim spot. Lock on. Putt to that spot — not the hole.
→ Doubt kills more putts than bad reads. Commit fully.
↗️ Double Breaker (rare) Toggle below the result card — OFF by default, auto-resets each hole. Walk the full putt, note steps at the inflection point (where slope changes). Enter S1 steps — S2 auto-calculates. Each segment gets its own slope, direction, and cup low. Triangle shows S1 (green) → inflection → S2 (gold) with combined overlay. Later inflection = more S2 weight, because the ball is slower near the cup.
03 · LOGGING
After the putt
10 seconds walking to the next tee. Don't skip it — this is where your pattern data comes from.
📏
Enter first putt distance
Tap +/− to set feet in 0.5ft increments. Separates lag problems (20ft+) from read problems (sub-10ft).
🔢
Tap putt count
1 (ACE) · 2 (GOOD) · 3 (3-PUTT) · 4+ (BLOW-UP). Reveals miss section automatically.
📍
Tap miss direction
Made · Left · Right · Short · Long. Most valuable data point. Feeds caddie memory and Round Insights panel.
➡️
Auto-advances to next hole
Moves forward in 1.6 seconds. Re-tap putt chips anytime to overwrite.
👥
Player 2 (optional)
After logging CVB's miss, a "Log [Player 2]" button appears. Tap it to log their putts on the same hole. Skip any hole — just advance. Doubles your data for faster formula validation.
Round survives everything Phone sleeps, browser closes — round is saved and restores exactly where you left off. Same calendar day only. Starts fresh tomorrow.
04 · ROUND INSIGHTS
What the app tracks
Open the scorecard at any point. Insights panel fires after 6+ holes of data to avoid false signals.
📊
Lag Putts (>30ft)
Count of lag situations. Triangle shows a 3ft halo on these — the target zone, not the hole.
🎯
Short Putt Conversion (≤8ft)
Made / attempted inside 8ft. Low conversion = read problem. Trust the triangle more on short putts.
↔️
Miss Bias
Left vs Right miss count. Systematic bias = alignment or face control issue, not a read issue.
⚡
Speed Control
Short vs Long miss count. Consistent short = dial up green speed. Consistent long = take pace off.
🧠
Caddie Memory (live)
After 3+ misses in the same direction, a compensation tip appears on your next putt read: "Pattern: missing LEFT — aim 2" more right." Requires 3 to fire — avoids false signals on small samples.
05 · PATTERNS
What your data tells you
After a few rounds one of these shows up clearly. That's your fix.
Short
Under-hitting lag putts. Dial up green speed one level, or switch from Die It to Normal.
Long
Charging downhill putts. Switch to Die It on fast greens. Aim to expire at the hole.
Left
Pulling, or over-reading left break. Drop slope one level on left-low putts. Check shoulder alignment.
Right
Pushing, or over-reading right break. Check face at impact. Verify aim point commitment.
3-putts 20ft+
Lag distance problem — not a read problem. Focus on the 3ft circle. Stop trying to make long putts.
3-putts 10ft−
Read problem. Trust the triangle more. You're second-guessing the aim point at address.
Grain Against
Consistently missing on against-grain putts? Add 10% to the aim point manually. The formula accounts for it but your eyes may be fighting the read.
Die It
Dying putts coming up short? You're decelerating, not dying. Make a committed stroke. Let the break do the work.
06 · THREE RULES
Non-negotiable
01Lag is the job on anything over 20ft
You are not trying to make it. You are trying to leave it inside 3 feet. The triangle gives you the line. Speed gives you the distance. The lag halo is your target — not the flag.
02Never be short
Die It means the ball barely crosses the hole — not that it dies before it gets there. Miss past the hole every time. Short is the worst miss in golf and the most common amateur miss.
03Commit to the aim point, not the hole
The app gives you a spot outside the cup. That is your target. Pick it, lock on, don't look at the hole again. The caddie memory and Chad tips exist so you aren't standing over the ball thinking — you already have the answer.
The doubt trap A committed wrong read often goes in. A doubted correct read often doesn't. Commit every time.
